Before Your Donation

  • Make sure you're eligible to donate blood.
  • Register an account.
  • Search for your nearest blood banks.
  • Schedule an appointment.

Blood transfusions are one of the most frequently performed procedures in hospitals and help treat patients suffering traumatic injuries, undergoing surgery, receiving treatment for cancer or blood disorders, as well as new mothers and premature babies.

COVID-19 Vaccinations?

In most cases, you can give blood after getting a COVID-19 vaccine if you are symptom-free and feeling well at the time of the donation! Ask your donation center for more information.

    WHAT IS IT

    • Blood donation is a selfless act of giving life to others in need by donating blood.
    • It's the process of voluntarily giving a portion of your blood to be used for transfusions or other medical procedures.
    • Blood donation plays a vital role in healthcare, as it helps save millions of lives each year.
    • By donating blood, you are contributing to your community and making a significant impact on someone's life.
    • Blood donation is a simple and safe process that takes just a few minutes, but its impact can last a lifetime.
